Restaurant Reviews

By Maryam Abbaz, Vishnu Eskew, and Conor Ruane

As Boston eases COVID-19 restrictions, more restaurants will become available to freely visit! Here are some of our favorites to check out this summer. And! You can see each of their location in the map at the bottom of the page.

Orinoco

Restaurant Description: Orinoco is a latin American eatery at Harvard square.

Best items:

  • Palmito is a salad with a heart of palm, with fresh greens and red onions,a bit of cabrales cheese vinaigrette,and bacon wrapped dates.
  • Costillitas includes boneless passion-fruit and barbeque baby-back ribs, with a bit of pickled onions and vinegar coleslaw.
  • Cachaca Pebellon is a Traditional corn pancake with mechada, tajadas, black beans, queso fresco.

Hokkaido Ramen Santouka

Restaurant Description: Hokkaido Ramen Santouka is a Japanese Eatery at Harvard square.

Best items:

  • Shoyu Ramen is a popular main dish that includes fresh ramen noodles, a hint of fish and soy sauce flavor, Chicken broth,and fermented bamboo.
  • Negimeshi is a well-known and popular side dish with white rice topped with katsuobushi, nori and diced green onions.
  • Shio Ramen is a specialty main dish at Hokkaido Ramen Santoukathat consists of ramen noodles at the base which has a mild salt flavor and is topped with a red plum.

Felipe's

Restaurant Description: Felipe's is a Mexican restaurant that became well known in Harvard Square for both its cheap prices and also the high quality of food, making it the ideal restaurant for the college kid on a budget and the wealthy couple on a night out. With a rooftop bar and plenty of seating inside, you can be sure that the restaurant will be full to the brim once in person dining resumes.

Best items:

  • Build your own burrito: Felipe offers a wide arrangement of items to put into your burritos from beef to vegetables, mild sauce to spicy sauce, and everything in between. Simply walk up to the counter, tell your server what you want in your food, and enjoy a wonderful tasting burrito.
  • Tortilla salad: If your looking to eat heatlthier, this is your dish, a salad with delicious torilla based croutons, in which you can put whatever toppings you want. I usually go with shrimp, corn, and refried beans, however the combinations are endless.

Boston Tea Shop

Restaurant Description: One of the first bubble tea shops in Harvard Square, I have been drinking here for almost an entire decade. The atmosphere inside the restaurant is wonderful, and the servers always greet you with a smile, this is the perfect place to be after a long day out in Harvard Square.

Best items:

  • Slushes: Choose from a variety of flavors to form your boba slush. On a hot summer day, a slush with boba is a must-have, keeping the heat away as you enjoy this delicious drink.
  • Mochi: If you are only looking for a small sweet-bite, look no further, because a mochi will have you covered. Choose from a wide array of flavors, and you will get a small ball of frozen ice cream with a wonderful texture and great taste.

Los Amigos Taqueria

Restaurant Description: Los Amigos Taqueria is a mid-sized, casual taco and burrito restaurant in Brighton Center. The popular taqueria is most busy in the evenings, around 6 pm, and is especially busy on a Friday evening. However, one great thing about Los Amigos is the swiftness at which they complete orders, giving their customers extremely low wait times. Their founder, Daniel, has successfully created a new fast-casual restaurant serving authentic Mexican cuisine with a contemporary twist, that has become a staple to Brighton Center. Los Amigos has become a successful restaurant chain with 5 locations in the Greater Boston Area, with the Brighton location being one of the most popular and highly acclaimed.

Best items:

  • Surf & Turf
  • Veggie Special
  • Chipotle Chicken
  • California
  • Coconut Shrimp

Thai North Restaurant

Restaurant Description: Thai North Restaurant is a cornerstone in the Brighton community and has been recognized for its outstanding Thai cuisine, excellent service, and friendly staff. The Thai North restaurant is known for its modern interpretation of classic dishes and its insistence on only using high-quality fresh ingredients. Thai North continues to bring the richness of Thai culture to Boston in the form of their quaint and small restaurant located in Oaks Square.

Best items:

  • Golden Treasures
  • Hot Mango Shrimp Specialty
  • Tofu Royale
  • Rainbow Paradise Entree
  • Kang Som Curry

Gyro City

Restaurant Description: Started by two Greek immigrants, Jimmy and Penny Christopher, Gyro City is modern Greek cuisine, serving authentic Greek-inspired dishes to the Boston area since 2012. Today, Paul (Jimmy’s son) and Penny run the three Gyro City locations, two in Boston and one in Providence. Jimmy currently resides in Athens where he continues to develop new and fascinating recipes. These recipes are passed down to his family in America that adopt them into their Gyro City menus. And of course, all of the food at Gyro City is handmade and prepared freshly every day.

Best items:

  • Bifteki Pita
  • Chicken Skewers
  • Falafel Dinner
  • Chicken Kabob Salad
  • Dolmades

Lulu’s Allston

Restaurant Description: Located in the always vibrant and dynamic Allston Village, Lulu’s Allston has a great variety of tasty dishes, including their world-renowned burgers. Lulu’s has been described as a charming eatery with midcentury-modern decor specializing in comfort foods with a unique twist. However, it might not be the greatest place to go for someone looking to shave off a couple of pounds, but if you’re looking for a filling and mouth-watering meal then Lulu’s Allston should be your top destination. Additionally, Lulu’s has a lengthy and unique brunch menu that is perfect for a late Sunday morning.

Best items:

  • Cajun Tots
  • Gazpacho
  • Lulus O.G. Smash Burger
  • Garlic Parm Smash Burger
  • Piña Bowl
